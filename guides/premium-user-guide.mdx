## Welcome, Premium User!

As a premium user, you have access to enhanced features and specialized equipment in the SWL Library Management System. This guide covers your exclusive privileges and how to maximize them.

## Premium User Privileges

Premium users enjoy several advantages over standard student accounts:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Specialized Equipment" icon="laptop">
    Access to advanced computing equipment and specialized accessories restricted from general users
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Multiple Laptops" icon="layer-group">
    Request multiple laptop units simultaneously for group projects or special needs
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Higher Limits" icon="gauge-high">
    Increased borrowing limits for accessories and equipment
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Priority Access" icon="star">

  </Card>

## Getting Started

### Account Setup

Premium accounts are created by system administrators. If you need premium access:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Request Premium Status">
    Contact a system administrator explaining your need for premium access
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Account Upgrade">
    Administrator will upgrade your existing account or create a new premium account
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Login">
    Use your document ID and password at `/auth/login`
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Access Premium Dashboard">
    You'll be redirected to `/dashboard` with premium features enabled
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Requesting Specialized Equipment

### Requesting Laptops (Enhanced)

Premium users can request multiple laptop units simultaneously:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Laptop Request">
    Go to `/request/laptop` from your dashboard
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Computing Equipment">
    Browse the full catalog including specialized computing equipment
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Specify Quantity">
    **Premium feature**: Enter the number of units needed (unlike students who are limited to 1)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Environment">
    Select usage location:
    - **Interno**: Library use only
    - **Externo**: External/take-home use
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit Request">
    Send for librarian approval
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
**Multiple Unit Requests:**
When requesting multiple laptops, the system reserves all units simultaneously. If insufficient units are available, the entire request will fail.
</Info>

### Accessing Specialized Accessories

Premium users have access to specialized computing accessories denied to regular students:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Accessory Request">
    Navigate to `/request/accessory`
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View Full Catalog">
    You'll see all available accessories including:
    - Specialized computing peripherals
    - Advanced technical equipment
    - General accessories
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Items and Quantity">
    Choose your items and specify quantity needed
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit">
    Request will go to librarian for approval
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Premium users still have a limit of **2 simultaneous accessory requests**. Plan your requests accordingly.
</Warning>

## Group Loan Management

### Managing Multiple Items

Premium users often manage multiple active loans simultaneously:

**Dashboard Organization:**
- All loans are displayed in chronological order (newest first)
- Each loan shows:
  - Item type and name
  - Request date and due date
  - Current status
  - Environment (interno/externo)
  - Penalty amount (if applicable)

**Status Types:**
- **Pendiente**: Awaiting approval
- **Activo**: Active loan in your possession
- **Atrasado**: Overdue - immediate action required
- **Devuelto**: Successfully returned
- **Rechazado**: Request denied

### Group Projects and Multiple Laptops

When requesting equipment for group work:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Calculate Needs">
    Determine exactly how many units your team requires
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check Availability">
    Verify sufficient units are available in the catalog before requesting
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit Single Request">
    Request all units in one transaction - they'll be reserved together
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Coordinate Pickup">
    After approval, coordinate with your team to pick up all units from the library desk
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Track Due Dates">
    All units from the same request share the same due date - plan group returns accordingly
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
**Pro Tip:** For large group projects, request equipment 2-3 days in advance to ensure approval and availability.
</Tip>

## Book Requests

Premium users request books the same way as students:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Browse Books">
    Navigate to `/request/book`
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Book">
    Choose from available titles in the catalog
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit and Pickup">
    After approval, collect the book from the library desk
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Book loans have a **15-day due date** and are subject to daily penalties if returned late (default: $5,000 COP/day).
</Note>

## Understanding Your Dashboard

### Loan Overview

Your premium dashboard at `/dashboard` provides:

- **Complete loan history**: All past and current requests
- **Status filtering**: Quickly find pending, active, or overdue items
- **Due date tracking**: Visual indicators for upcoming due dates
- **Penalty tracking**: Real-time calculation of overdue fees for books

### Monitoring Active Loans

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="View Active Items">
    Filter your dashboard to see only 'activo' status loans - these are items currently in your possession.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Check Due Dates">
    Due dates are displayed in Colombia time (UTC-5). Items approaching due dates should be prioritized for return.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Track Penalties">
    For book loans, the penalty amount updates daily if overdue. Return books promptly to minimize fees.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Review Observations">
    If a request is rejected, check the observation field for the librarian's reason.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Best Practices for Premium Users

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Batch Your Requests">
    When requesting multiple units, submit them in a single request rather than multiple separate requests for better tracking.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Communicate Project Timelines">
    If you need equipment for extended periods, communicate with the librarian before the due date to request extensions.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Return as a Batch">
    When returning multiple units from the same request, return them all together to simplify processing.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Monitor Specialized Equipment">
    Computing equipment often has limited availability. Request as soon as you know you'll need it.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Use External Wisely">
    When requesting 'externo' use, ensure you can safely transport and secure the equipment off-campus.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Track Team Responsibility">
    When borrowing for groups, the premium user who requested the items is ultimately responsible for their return.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Fast Loan Feature Access

Premium users may be granted access to fast loan features:

- When using fast loan (`/fast_loan`), premium users see specialized equipment catalogs
- General category items are excluded from premium fast loans
- Same quantity and environment rules apply

<Info>
Fast loan is typically used by librarians for in-person requests, but premium users may have access for special scenarios.
</Info>

## Upgrading to Premium: What Changes?

When upgrading from student to premium:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Role Change">
    Administrator updates your role from 'cliente' to 'premium'
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enhanced Access">
    Immediately gain access to:
    - Specialized computing equipment
    - Multi-unit laptop requests
    - Full accessory catalog
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Same Limits Apply">
    Note that some limits remain:
    - Cannot have duplicate active laptop requests
    - Maximum 2 simultaneous accessories (but from full catalog)
    - Standard due dates (15 days)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I request more than 2 accessories?">
    No, the system enforces a maximum of 2 simultaneous accessory requests for all users, including premium accounts. However, premium users can access specialized accessories unavailable to students.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What's the maximum number of laptops I can request?">
    While premium users can request multiple laptop units in a single request, you cannot have overlapping active laptop requests. Quantity is limited by availability.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Why doesn't the fast loan show all items?">
    Premium users in fast loan see only specialized/computing equipment. General items are filtered out to streamline the interface.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can I transfer items to another user?">
    No, loans are tied to the requesting user. The borrower must return items personally to the library desk.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Do premium users get longer loan periods?">
    No, all users have the same 15-day standard loan period. Extensions must be requested from the librarian before the due date.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
